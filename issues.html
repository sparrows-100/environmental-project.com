Environmental Issues Facing Uganda

Uganda’s environment is under increasing pressure due to human activities, population growth, and climate change. These challenges affect livelihoods, biodiversity, and sustainable development.

Deforestation

Large areas of forest are cleared for charcoal burning, timber, farming, and settlement.

Effects: Loss of wildlife habitats, reduced rainfall, soil erosion, climate change, and scarcity of firewood.

Climate Change

Climate change has caused unpredictable weather patterns including prolonged droughts, intense rainfall, and rising temperatures.

Effects: Crop failure, food insecurity, water shortages, flooding, and increased poverty.

Water Pollution

Rivers and lakes are polluted by industrial waste, untreated sewage, agricultural chemicals, and plastic waste.

Effects: Waterborne diseases, death of aquatic life, unsafe drinking water, and reduced fish stocks.

Wetland Destruction

Wetlands are drained for farming, construction, and industrial development despite their importance to the ecosystem.

Effects: Increased flooding, loss of biodiversity, water purification failure, and climate imbalance.

Loss of Biodiversity

Many plant and animal species are threatened due to habitat destruction, pollution, and illegal hunting.

Effects: Species extinction, ecosystem imbalance, reduced tourism revenue, and loss of medicinal plants.

Overfishing

Excessive and illegal fishing practices reduce fish populations in major lakes like Lake Victoria.

Effects: Decline in fish stocks, loss of income, food insecurity, and ecosystem disruption.

Encroachment on Protected Areas

Human settlement and farming activities are expanding into national parks and forest reserves.

Effects: Human-wildlife conflict, habitat loss, reduced tourism, and biodiversity decline.

Urbanization Pressure

Rapid urban growth without proper planning increases pollution and strains infrastructure.

Effects: Traffic congestion, poor sanitation, air pollution, and increased disease outbreaks.

Land Degradation

Poor farming methods, overgrazing, and deforestation degrade soil quality.

Effects: Reduced crop yields, desertification, poverty, and food insecurity.

Plastic Pollution

Improper disposal of plastic waste is common in urban and rural areas.

Effects: Blocked drainage, flooding, wildlife deaths, and long-term soil contamination.

Indoor Air Pollution

Many households rely on firewood and charcoal for cooking in poorly ventilated spaces.

Effects: Respiratory diseases, eye problems, and premature deaths.

Flooding

Flooding is becoming more frequent due to climate change and poor drainage systems.

Effects: Destruction of homes, loss of lives, crop damage, and disease outbreaks.

Drought

Extended dry periods affect water supply and agricultural productivity.

Effects: Hunger, livestock deaths, water scarcity, and increased poverty.

Mining and Quarrying

Unregulated mining activities damage land and pollute water sources.

Effects: Land degradation, water contamination, health risks, and ecosystem destruction.
